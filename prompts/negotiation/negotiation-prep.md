## Purpose
Prepare for a negotiation by clarifying interests, anticipating the other side, and setting a plan
before terms are discussed. Built on interest-based (win-win) negotiation, not positional haggling.

## Inputs
- **deal_summary** — what's on the table (scope, value, timing).
- **our_goals** — what Eik & Friends wants, ranked (must-have vs nice-to-have).
- **their_likely_goals** — what we believe the other side wants and fears.
- **constraints** — hard limits on either side (budget, dates, capacity, policy).
- **leverage** — what strengthens our position (and theirs).
- **walk_away** — our BATNA / the point at which we'd rather not do the deal.

## Prompt
> You are coaching Jonathan Foss (Sales Manager, Eik & Friends) before a negotiation. Use an
> interest-based approach: expand value before dividing it; protect the relationship.
>
> Deal: {{deal_summary}}
> Our goals (ranked): {{our_goals}}
> Their likely goals/fears: {{their_likely_goals}}
> Constraints: {{constraints}}
> Leverage: {{leverage}}
> Walk-away / BATNA: {{walk_away}}
>
> Produce a prep sheet:
> 1. **Interests behind positions** — ours and theirs, separating wants from underlying needs.
> 2. **Trade space** — what we can give that's cheap for us but valuable to them, and vice versa.
> 3. **Anchoring** — a principled opening position and the rationale to justify it.
> 4. **Concession plan** — the order we'd concede in, and what we'd ask for in return each time.
> 5. **Likely objections** — and a calm, value-based response to each.
> 6. **Walk-away line** — restate it clearly so it's not crossed under pressure.
> 7. **Opening move** — the first thing to say to set a collaborative tone.
>
> Be specific and realistic. Flag anything where we lack information.

## Notes & variations
- Never concede without getting something in return — trade, don't give.
- Protect the relationship; we want repeat business and referrals, not a won battle.
- If walk-away is unclear, resolve that **before** the negotiation — it's the anchor for everything.

## Example
A corporate event deal where the client pushes on price → prep sheet that trades a lower headline
price for a firm date and a multi-event commitment, with a clear floor below which we walk.
